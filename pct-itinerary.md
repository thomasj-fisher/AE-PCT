## How to use this file

1. **Daily progress** table (bottom) — Athena reports in, you fill `Actual mile` and `Actual location`.
2. `Δ days` = (actual day to reach mile X) − (expected day to reach mile X). Positive = behind, negative = ahead.
3. When considering a flight: use **Pickup logistics** to pick the segment she's in on your target dates, then book to the listed airport.
4. Re-baseline if she drifts >5 days: take her latest actual mile, find the new expected day for the Northern Terminus, and shift remaining expected dates.

## Notes & open items

- **Cleef campsite** — not a standard PCT waypoint name. Confirm exact mile with Athena (likely somewhere mile 5–15: Hauser Cyn, Lake Morena, or a dispersed site). Update Day 1 row.
- **Daily expected miles** between resupply points are linear interpolation. Real PCT daily progress varies with terrain (Sierra and Goat Rocks days will be slower, NorCal/Oregon flat sections faster).
- **Sierra snow / fire closures** — June dates assume an average snow year. Recheck snow conditions in May; she may need to flip-flop. Keep an eye on fire season for OR/WA late summer.
- **Stehekin → Manning Park final stretch** is the most logistics-heavy — the only road exit is the trailhead at Hwy 3, and the closest US airport is EAT via Chelan. Many hikers' family meets them at Manning Park Lodge, then drives Hwy 3 west to YVR.
- **Re-baseline trigger**: if `Δ days` exceeds ±5, shift all remaining expected dates by the same amount.
